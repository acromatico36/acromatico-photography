How to Get the Perfect Family Beach Sunrise Photos

Have you ever looked at a photo of a beach sunrise and thought, “I want to take a picture like that!”?  With a little planning and a lot of patience, you can get the perfect family beach sunrise photos.

Here are a few tips to make it happen:

1,  Choose your location. You’ll want to find a spot with a good view of the sun as it rises. 

2.  Wake up early! This is the most important step – you’ll want to be at your chosen location at least 30 minutes before sunrise. 

3.  Setup your camera, tripod or light.

4.  Wait for the sun to rise. 

5.  Finally, don’t forget to enjoy the moment!

If you’re looking to hire a professional photographer, by following these simple tips, you’ll be sure to get a stunning photo of the perfect family beach sunrise.

It all starts with the perfect location

 When it comes to getting the perfect family beach sunrise photo, the location is key. You’ll want to find a spot with a clear view of the horizon so you can clearly see the sun as it rises. If there are trees or buildings in the way, it may block the sun and ruin your shot. Consider the time of year when you’ll be taking your photo. If you’re planning to take your photo, you’ll want to make sure the sun will be in the right place. Research the exact sunrise time In the city that you’re planning on shooting, on the date that you’re looking to take your family beach sunrise photos.

The early bird gets the shot

Waking up early is the most important step in getting the perfect family beach sunrise photo. You’ll want to be at your chosen location at least 30 minutes before sunrise. This will give you time to set up your location, and your photographer will get setup with their camera and take a few test shots. The good thing about hiring a professional photographer Is that they’ll be ready for that sunrise hour, with their lights and right lenses, to produce an artistic shot of your family. 

Framing is everything

Once you have your camera set up, it’s time to start taking photos. But before you start snapping away, take a moment to think about framing. If you want to include the whole family in the photo, you’ll need to make sure everyone fits in the frame. You’ll also want to pay attention to the background. A busy background can distract from the main subject of the photo, which should be the sunrise. So try to find a spot with a plain background, like the sky or the ocean. 

Capturing the moment

 As the sun starts to rise, you’ll want to start taking photos. But don’t just take any photo—try to capture the feeling of the moment. The best way to do this is to take a series of photos, so you can choose the one that best captures the feeling of the sunrise. To get a sweeping view of the sunrise, you can take a panorama. Or, if you want to focus on the sun itself, you can try taking a close-up. Just remember to keep your camera steady, as even the slightest movement can ruin a close-up photo if you’re not using flash or strobe lights.

Don’t forget the details

Once you have your main photo, it’s time to start thinking about the details. What kind of photos will tell the story of your sunrise experience? You might want to take a photo of the shadows the sun is casting, or the way the light reflects off the water. Or, if you’re taking a photo of your family, you might want to capture a candid moment.

Use natural light

One of the best things about sunrise photos is the natural light. The light at sunrise is unlike any other time of day, so you’ll want to make sure you take advantage of it. To get the most beautiful light, try taking your photos in the first few minutes after the sun rises. This is when the light is at its best. 

And…ACTION!

Now that you know how to take the perfect family beach sunrise photo, it’s time to get out there and start taking pictures! Just remember to have fun and enjoy the moment.  And by all means, we recommend hiring a professional photographer at least once per year, to capture your beautiful growing family year after year.

You’re building moments and memories that last for a lifetime.  And there’s no better way of doing It than by capturing the perfect family beach sunrise photos with your professional photographer.
